Euler's totient function, ϕ(n)\phi(n) [sometimes called the phi function], is used to determine the number of positive numbers less than or equal to nn which are relatively prime to nn. For example, as 1,2,4,5,71, 2, 4, 5, 7, and 88, are all less than nine and relatively prime to nine, ϕ(9)=6\phi(9)=6.
The number 11 is considered to be relatively prime to every positive number, so ϕ(1)=1\phi(1)=1.

Interestingly, ϕ(87109)=79180\phi(87109)=79180, and it can be seen that 8710987109 is a permutation of 7918079180.
